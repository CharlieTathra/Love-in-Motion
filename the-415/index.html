The route

Attunga → Sydney Cricket Ground · ~484 km over 13 days · finish Mon 4 Jan 2027 (Pink Test)

DRAFT — this is the safety-audited line: south down the New England Highway to the Hunter, then the coast through Newcastle, the Fernleigh Track and the Central Coast, and the Old Pacific Highway into Sydney — the safest legal route (the M1 can’t be run). Towns and daily distances are the plan; the audit’s ⚠ items still need a route-planner / Street View / on-ground check, and each night’s stay booked, before locking. (“The 415” is the campaign name — the mapped route measures about 484 km.)
    ⚠️ Biggest safety checks (from the audit)

    Day 2 (Tamworth → Wallabadah, ~54 km) is the longest and least-mapped — a Street View pass and all-day vehicle shadowing are essential. Chainage-mark the shoulder gaps on Days 2–4 and 6; keep Day 7 off the Hunter Expressway (a motorway — verified) and confirm the Hexham bridge pinch (Day 8). The Hexham crossing (Day 8) has shared paths and the Hawkesbury crossing (Day 12) has a footpath (Peats Ferry Bridge) — both confirmed walkable. Everything from Newcastle south is well protected — Fernleigh Track, coastal shared paths and the Old Pacific Highway.

    Estimated ~484 km, finishing at the Sydney Cricket Ground on Monday 4 January 2027, in time for the Pink Test — via the Old Pacific Highway, Hornsby and the Harbour Bridge walkway. Full detail and the verify list are in the route safety audit and route book below.
